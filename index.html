Government grants fund critical services, but reimbursements can take months. ReFund provides upfront, zero-cost recoverable grants so nonprofits can focus on their mission, not their cash flow.

Partner With Us

The Cash Flow Crisis

Critical community services are stalled by funding mechanisms that weren't built for the organizations delivering them.

The Reality

Government grants typically reimburse expenses only after costs are incurred. This model forces nonprofits to act as banks for the government.

60-180
Days delay in reimbursement is routine

The Consequence

Organizations lacking reserves must cut programs or seek high-cost debt financing to survive the gap.

12%
Of grant value lost to interest & fees

Why It Matters

Resources Lost

Money intended for community impact is diverted to lenders through interest payments and financing fees.

Equity Gap Widens

Frontline organizations, often led by BIPOC leaders, are most excluded from traditional financing options.

Efficiency Reduced

Administrative burden and financial stress reduce the operational capacity of vital nonprofits.

Testimonial

"BIPOC-led organizations, especially Indigenous nonprofits, face a cruel irony: we're awarded government grants because our communities have the greatest need, but we're denied financing because we have the least reserves."

Albert Brian Wallace

CEO, Indigenous Futures Society

The Solution

ReFund makes it possible for nonprofits to access government funding by providing upfront capital to cover eligible costs until reimbursements arrive.

How ReFund Works

1

Funders Contribute

Funders make a recoverable grant to ReFund pool.

2

Upfront Access

ReFund provides upfront capital to nonprofits with contracts.

3

Repayment

Nonprofits repay ReFund once government pays them.

4

Recycle

Funds are recycled for future use or returned to funders.
