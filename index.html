The Cash Flow Crisis

Nonprofits providing critical programs and services rely on reimbursement-based funding mechanisms that don't work for them.

The Reality

Government grants typically reimburse expenses only after costs are incurred. Nonprofits must front these costs.

60-180
Days delay in reimbursement is routine

The Consequence

Organizations must scale back critical work, seek high-cost financing, or use precious unrestricted funding to cover the gap.

12%
Of grant value lost to interest & fees

Why It Matters

Resources Lost

Money that should benefit communities is diverted to funders and lenders through interest payments and financing fees.

Equity Gap Widens

Small, BIPOC-led organizations often have limited cash flow and may be excluded from traditional financing options.

Efficiency Reduced

Administrative burden and financial stress reduce the operational capacity of nonprofits.

Testimonial

"BIPOC-led organizations, especially Indigenous nonprofits, face a cruel irony: we're awarded government grants because our communities have the greatest need, but we're denied financing because we have the least reserves."

Albert Brian Wallace

CEO, Indigenous Futures Society

The Solution

ReFund makes it possible for nonprofits to access government funding by providing upfront capital to cover eligible costs until reimbursements arrive.

How ReFund Works

1

Funder Contributions

Funders make a traditional or recoverable grant to ReFund.

2

Recoverable Grants

ReFund provides funds to nonprofits with government grants and contracts.

3

Repayment

Nonprofits repay ReFund once government pays them.

4

Recycling

Funds are recycled for future use or returned to funders.
