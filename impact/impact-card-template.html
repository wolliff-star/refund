Green Valley Conservation Corps Oregon

By bridging the gap between federal grants and immediate cash flow needs, we empowered local action to protect vital ecosystems.

The Challenge

Green Valley Conservation Corps was awarded a $500,000 federal grant to restore critical watershed areas in Southern Oregon. The project required immediate action to meet environmental deadlines, but the EPA's reimbursement process meant waiting 90 days for payment.

With less than $50,000 in reserves, the organization couldn't afford to front the costs. Traditional lenders offered loans at 9% interest, which would have cost $45,000—nearly 10% of the grant value. These funds would have come from unrestricted reserves meant for organizational resilience and future programs.

The Solution

ReFund provided a $500,000 recoverable grant, allowing Green Valley to begin work immediately. The zero-cost, zero-interest grant covered:

  • Equipment rental and supplies for restoration work
  • Seasonal worker salaries for 12 local staff
  • Project management costs
  • Environmental monitoring and reporting

The organization repaid ReFund in full once EPA reimbursement arrived 87 days later, ensuring the project proceeded without financial strain.

The Impact

With immediate access to capital, Green Valley Conservation Corps achieved remarkable results:

  • Restored 50 miles of critical watershed habitat
  • Hired 12 seasonal workers from local communities
  • Completed the project on schedule and within budget
  • Saved $45,000 that would have gone to loan interest
  • Preserved unrestricted reserves for future opportunities
  • Established a strong track record for future federal grants
